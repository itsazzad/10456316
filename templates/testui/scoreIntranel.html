Pronunciation

Pronunciation refers to our ability to produce individual sounds correctly. If there is a sound in English which your native language does not have, you will probably have trouble with it.

OK

Intonation

Intonation is variation in spoken pitch. It involves dividing speech into thought groups, focusing on particular words, and using a pitch movement. These differences distinguish questions from statements, focus attention on new information, and express speaker emotions.

OK

Pauses

It is natural for speech to have some pauses. They divide speech into thought groups and can highlight important information. Too many long pauses which are inappropriately placed can affect the fluency and comprehensibility of your speech.

OK

Speed

Individual speakers differ from others in their natural rate of speech. If your speech is too slow or too fast, it may interfere with your comprehensibility.

OK

Vocabulary Mastery

Vocabulary mastery refers to mispronunciations of words which are likely due to their being unfamiliar than mispronunciations of sounds (word stress errors, hesitations, etc.).

OK

Range of Vocabulary

Range of vocabulary refers to the level, variety, and relevance of words chosen for self-expression.

OK

Grammar Mastery

Grammar mastery refers to the grammatical correctness of your sentences.

OK

Sentence Structure

Sentence structures refers to the length and complexity of sentences you use.

For example, below one complex sentence expresses the same meaning as the three simple sentences but does it in a more concise and fluent manner.

"I did the laundry. I cleaned the house. I was talking to my friend on the phone all the time."

"I did the laundry and cleaned the house while talking to my friend on the phone."

OK

Comprehension

Listening comprehension refers to our ability to extract explicit and implicit information from other people’s speech. It involves understanding the meaning of individual words and sentences but also the purpose and attitudes of the speaker.

OK

Transcription

Transcription refers to our ability to understand and reproduce the information we hear. Comparing the original sentences to what you wrote will help you to identify where your challenges lie: is it segmenting the stream of speech into words, hearing differences between sounds, or simply knowing the words?

OK
